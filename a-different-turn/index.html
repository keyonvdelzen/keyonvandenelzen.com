A different turn.

July 17, 2025

I woke up around 5. Or maybe a bit before.
I don’t remember exactly, but I do remember checking the time — just past 5 a.m.
Too early, I thought.
I had hoped to sleep a little longer, especially since I really need more sleep lately.
I stayed in bed for a while.
Just lying there. Thinking.
Letting my mind pull me wherever it wanted to go — though gently steering it when it drifted too far off into the wild. As it tends to do.
I thought about a bunch of things, most of which I’ve already forgotten.
And it’s only been 90 minutes since I was there, thinking.

After half an hour or so, I figured that was enough lying awake.
I got up. Opened the blinds.
The world was waking.
Pastel sky. Birds chirping.
Lovely.
Shoes on. Keys, water bottle.
Quick bathroom pit stop.
Then I stepped outside. Just a little walk.
It soothes my soul, going for a walk in the morning.

I thought I’d take the usual route — toward the church, down the little street to the right, into the park.
But I didn’t.
Instead of turning right, I went straight.
Toward the town square.
A good change.
Makes life more memorable, taking a different turn every once in a while.
Deliberately doing something different from what is routinized.
I like that.

"Good morning!"
Thursday today — market day.
And early in the morning the vendors start setting up their little stands in the square.
There was only one guy there when I arrived. Still early. Maybe 5:30?
I greeted him and he shouted back a cheerful “Good morning!”
Lovely.
I love market people.
They’re always so cheerful, open, warm.
Okay not always, fair — some are cranky too.

I kept walking.
Past the town square.
Past the renovated old folks’ homes.
Right turn.
Toward where the old sports complex used to be.
(It got torn down a few years ago — there are houses there now.)
Then another right.
Eventually, I made it to the park. But this time, from a different direction.
Good change of pace. Makes life more memorable.

I wandered through the park a little. But I noticed something in me — a pull.
I wanted to go back home.
Sit behind my PC. Write. Have some coffee. Wake Nala. Carry her upstairs. Put her beside me on the bed.
She’s next to me now.
I’m behind my PC.
Writing. Having coffee.
It’s good.
Life is good.
This morning’s been good.
I was strolling around earlier with a smile from ear to ear.
And now, I feel at peace.
