A reflection on trauma loops, recovery, and the human experience.

July 10, 2025

It’s strange how much clarity can come from simply having the right words. For years, I’ve struggled with what felt like an invisible trap: I’d quit smoking weed or cigarettes, start eating cleaner, lifting more, walking more, doing more — only to eventually find myself spiraling again. Fatigue would build. Focus would fade. Anxiety would grow. And eventually, I’d crash or give in and start numbing again.

But now I'm starting to understand something deeper. And for the first time, I’m capturing it in words. Something called the "neuro-somatic trauma loop". It’s not just psychological. It’s physical. When I get tired or feel off, my body interprets it as a signal that collapse is coming. Because… in the past, it was. My nervous system has memorized what burnout felt like — and now, when I feel similar sensations, it launches into overdrive, releasing adrenaline, trying to protect me from going there again.

The past leaks into the present. The body remembers, even when the mind has moved on. My nervous system can’t distinguish between past and present, it treats similar sensations as if they’re happening again, rather than just now. My system doesn’t see now as now. It sees now as then. And even though I’m in a different place, in a different time, my body hasn’t caught up yet. It still flinches when I look tired in the mirror. It still panics when my energy dips. That’s the loop.

And this whole thing is becoming increasingly clear to me. It's a misaligned protective mechanism.

So now, I’m approaching it differently.
I give my mind and body permission to pause — panoramic walks, deep breaths, long stares at trees and sky.
I remind myself: this isn’t 2018. Or 2019. Or any of the other burnouts. This is now. And I’m not the same.
I meet the early signals with curiosity instead of fear: “Ah. There’s that sensation. Hello again.”
I stay devoted to the slow work of untying — one knot at a time. Disentangling. It’s my life’s mission.

In August I’m going all in.
☕ No caffeine.
💧 A water fast to start.
🥩 Continuing my shift to a carnivore lifestyle.
🛌 Prioritizing sleep, rest, and rituals.
🌾 Stepping back from the need to perform.
🚶 Walking. Being. Listening.

I’m letting my system relearn what safety feels like.
And I’ll keep walking — literally and figuratively — through these forests, through this healing, with reverence.

It’s complex.
It’s beautiful.
And I love being human.
