On “Phoneless Fridays”, the smartphone, and living life.

August 9th, 2025

Did a phoneless Friday evening yesterday. For the second time. It was phenomenal.

The things that emerge in the absence of boundless distraction are beyond valuable. Thoughts. Emotions. Ideas. Memories.

I would want to live like that every day. But the downside would be that I wouldn't be able to make use of the modern marvel that is the smartphone. I would especially miss not being able to post to my private Insta log.

But also not having access to the information that a smartphone provides. And the tools. Planning. Noting things down. Logging. Different apps: macro nutrient tracker, Hevy the workout app, and so many others.

So, I think there's a balance to be struck. And for now, doing phoneless Friday evenings seems to be a good first step towards that balance.

I wrote this on paper by the way. A blank sheet. Sitting at the kitchen table of my mom's place. I just cycled here from home — which is Den Bosch now. I've been living there for a week. I brought some dirty laundry with me in my backpack and put it in the laundry machine here.

Yesterday evening I cycled, and walked, and sat. I did some thinking and writing and drawing on project ideas I've had for my new place — my sacred sleep cave, for example, and a modular storage closet-cabinet-bookcase. Life is good. I love life.

For the rest of my time on this beautiful globe, I will pursue becoming as good as I can become at this thing we call “living”. Living life. Being good to family, friends, to people. Honing my skills. Working on the self. Forging strength. I will try my best. In every imaginable dimension. Face life's challenges head-on.
